Bangladesh Cricket Board

The Bangladesh men's national cricket team, popularly known as The Tigers, is administered by the Bangladesh Cricket Board. It is a Full Member of the International Cricket Council with Test.

The Bangladesh Cricket board (Bengali: বাংলাদেশ ক্রিকেট বোর্ড; abbreviated as BCB) is the governing body of cricket in Bangladesh. The Bangladesh Cricket Board first became an associate of the International Cricket Council in 1977[2] and on June 26, 2000 became a Full Member..=[3] BCB have three International Cricket teams which represent Bangladesh in international cricket, which are Bangladesh Men's national cricket team, Bangladesh women's national cricket team and Bangladesh under-19 cricket team. Regional Cricket AssociationsRegional Cricket Associations:

In the 2022 Annual General Meeting, BCB President Nazmul Hasan Papon announced to have amended the board's constitution to make way for the Regional Cricket Associations. Mr. Papon, who promised to make decentralisation of country's cricket through the creation of Regional bodies, back in 2013, when first time he got elected as the President of the cricket board. Finally through the AGM 2022, he announced to create seven regional bodies in the division of Barisal, Chattogram, Dhaka, Khulna, Rajshahi, Rangpur and Sylhet. While Mymensingh has been kept under the Dhaka division. The regional bodies which are comparatively bigger will have 11 members while smaller bodies will consist of 7 members.[11][12]
